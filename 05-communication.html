Essential Course Skill

Technical Self-Sufficiency & Communication

In BCIS 1110, earning a good grade requires mastering technical concepts, but real success in college and modern business requires **technical self-sufficiency** and **professional digital etiquette**.

1. Professional Digital Communication

Mastering structured, polite, and effective emails to instructors and colleagues, as well as maintaining constructive, analytical dialogue in online discussion forums without conflict.

  • Clear subject lines & section numbers
  • Actionable details & professional tone
  • Civil, evidence-based discussion board replies

2. Technical Adaptation & Troubleshooting

Navigating software and operating system differences across Windows, Mac, and Chromebooks, while using resources like the CNM Virtual Desktop (`virtualdesktop.cnm.edu`) to resolve issues independently.

  • Using CNM Virtual Desktop on non-Windows devices
  • Creating screenshots & documenting error logs
  • Backup contingency plans prior to deadline cutoffs
