AI Ethics & Information Literacy Task

Intent Architecting: The Dangers of "Poor Wishes" & AI Hallucination

In modern business and computing, Generative AI acts like a literal genie: it grants exactly what you request, not necessarily what you meant. Poorly constructed prompts ("poor wishes") produce subtle hallucinations, factual distortions, and dangerous organizational bias.

Target Concept
Intent Architecting

Transforming vague requests into structured, constrained, and verifiable prompts that force AI models to produce accurate, unbiased outputs.

Core Skill Evaluated
Hallucination Critique

Cross-referencing AI outputs against trusted academic & enterprise sources (SIMnet, O365, CNM Library Databases) to catch false citations.

Primary Tools Used
Microsoft Office 365 & Copilot

Using Office 365 apps, Microsoft Copilot/ChatGPT, CNM Library EBSCO/Gale databases, and Brightspace LMS.

Real-World Business Case Study

The Case of the "Poor Wish" at Sol Southwest Logistics

Location: Albuquerque, NM | Role: Junior Business Operations Analyst

You recently landed an entry-level position as an Operations Specialist at Sol Southwest Logistics, a growing regional shipping and warehousing company based in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Your supervisor, Alex, needs an urgent market assessment report on implementing automated electric cargo fleets for regional delivery across New Mexico and West Texas.

The "Poor Wish" Incident

Pressed for time, a former intern drafted a prompt in ChatGPT/Copilot asking:

"Write me a 1-page report proving that switching our delivery trucks to electric vehicles in New Mexico will save us 40% in costs this year, and list 3 local NM laws that mandate this transition."

The AI gave the intern exactly what they asked for—a convincing, polished report filled with fabricated statutes (such as the made-up "New Mexico Clean Freight Act of 2024"), false cost projections, and completely invented local utility incentives.

Why This is a Dangerous "Poor Wish"

In AI theory, this is the classic Monkey’s Paw / Poor Wish failure. The prompt was flawed in three critical ways:

  • Confirmation Bias
    Demanded a Specific Outcome

    The prompt forced the AI to "prove 40% savings" rather than objectively evaluating financial feasibility.

  • Prompt Hallucination
    Assumed Law Existence

    It instructed the AI to "list 3 statutes," causing the LLM to invent plausible-sounding legal citations to fulfill the request.

  • Missing Verification
    Zero Guardrails or Sources

    It provided no constraints, role persona, geographic boundary, or requirement to cite verified primary sources.

Your Role & Mission

Alex caught the errors before the executive meeting and has reassigned the project to you. As a BCIS 1110 student trained in Information Literacy, your mission is to deconstruct the "poor wish," refactor the prompt using professional Intent Architecting techniques, perform empirical source verification via CNM Libraries/SIMnet, and present an ethical audit report.

Instructional Workflow

Four-Phase Research & Refactoring Task

Follow these steps sequentially. You can use the embedded Interactive Workbench in Section 4 to test your prompts before drafting your final submission document.

1

Phase 1: Critique the "Poor Wish" Output

SLO 3 Focus

Run the original vague prompt in Copilot, ChatGPT, or the Interactive Workbench (Section 4). Document at least two distinct hallucinations (e.g., fabricated law citation, unrealistic percentage claim, or false local policy).

Required Action: Identify the logical fallback where the model fills missing context with plausible sounding falsehoods. Explain why this occurs technically (LLMs predicting tokens rather than retrieving verified facts).
2

Phase 2: Perform "Intent Architecting" Refactoring

Prompt Engineering

Rewrite the prompt using the C-P-C-V Architectural Framework (Context, Persona, Constraints, Verification). Your refactored prompt must eliminate confirmation bias.

C - Context Logistics firm in NM
P - Persona Unbiased Business Analyst
C - Constraints Specify risks, costs, state laws
V - Verification Require citation sources
3

Phase 3: Source Verification via CNM Libraries / Academic Databases

SLO 3 Verification

Use the CNM Library Databases (e.g., EBSCOhost, Business Source Complete) or official state government websites (.gov) to find one real, verified fact or statistic regarding electric vehicle fleet adoption or incentives in New Mexico.

Comparison Rule: Contrast the AI’s generated claim against the real source document. Highlight discrepancies in numbers, scope, or applicability.
4

Phase 4: Synthesize & Draft Academic/Business Report

SLO 1 & 2 Ethics

Compile your findings into Microsoft Word (via your CNM O365 account) or paste into the Brightspace Submission Template provided in Section 6. Include full AI prompt transparency disclosure as required by CNM AI Policy.

CNM Academic AI Integrity & Citation Guidance

Central New Mexico Community College Academic Honesty Standards

Policy Grade: Authorized Activity Guidance

Permitted & Encouraged AI Use

In BCIS 1110, Generative AI tools are permitted specifically when explicitly directed by the assignment. You are encouraged to use AI as a brain-storming partner, prompt refactoring sandbox, and critique subject.

Citation & Transparency Mandate

Passing off AI-generated text as your own unassisted writing without full disclosure constitutes academic dishonesty. Always attach the CNM AI Transparency Log detailing prompt strings, model name, and version date.
